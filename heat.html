How is heat different from Temprature?

The core difference is that heat deals with thermal energy, whereas temperature is more concerned with molecular kinetic energy. Heat is the transfer of thermal energy, whereas temperature is a property the object exhibits.

Transfer of heat:
Heat transfers from hot to cold.

Types of transfer of heat:
1. Conduction: Heat conduction (or thermal conduction) is the movement of heat from one object to another one that has different temperature when they are touching each other
2. Convection: Convective heat transfer is the transfer of heat between two bodies by currents of moving gas or fluid. In free convection, air or water moves away from the heated body as the warm air or water rises and is replaced by a cooler parcel of air or water
3. Radiation: Thermal radiation, process by which energy, in the form of electromagnetic radiation, is emitted by a heated surface in all directions and travels directly to its point of absorption at the speed of light; thermal radiation does not require an intervening medium to carry it.
